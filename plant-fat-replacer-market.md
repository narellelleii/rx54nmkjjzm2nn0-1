<p><p>The plant fat replacer market consists of three primary types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based alternatives. Carbohydrate-based replacers, such as modified starches and fibers, provide textural qualities and moisture retention. Protein-based options, derived from sources like soy and pea, offer emulsifying properties and enhance nutritional value. Lipid-based replacers, which include various plant oils and fats, mimic traditional fats in flavor and texture, enhancing mouthfeel while maintaining a plant-based profile, catering to health-conscious and environmentally aware consumers.</p></p>
